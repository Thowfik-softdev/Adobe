How to use Generative Fill in Photoshop.

Use any selection tool to select an object or area in your image. Select the Generative Fill button in the Contextual Task Bar that appears.

Write a prompt describing the object or scene you want to generate in the text-entry prompt box. Or, leave it blank and Photoshop will fill in the selection based on the surroundings

Click Generate. You'll see thumbnail previews of variations generated based on your prompt and a Generative Layer will be created in your Layers panel for non-destructive editing of your original image.

Tip: To generate even more options, click Generate again. You can also try editing your prompt to fine tune your results.

Turn nothing into something faster than ever.

Add more to anything, anywhere on your image.

Expand images to turn a vertical shot into a wide horizontal image, change a background, update an outfit or even add a generated objects to an image with a short description.

High-quality creations, low time commitment.

Save hours of time bringing photorealistic or surrealistic ideas to life. Generative Fill adds and creates with appropriate shadows, reflections, lighting and perspective to give you stunning results that need much less editing.

Refine, retouch, rework.

Add yellow lines to pavement or a wistful mist to an image with a text prompt and then make adjustments. Generated content is added on a new layer so you can edit, refine or remove any creation without damaging the original image.

Cut out unwanted elements with ease.

Remove stray shadows from a photo or trim an extra tree from a generated AI image quickly. Simply select what you want to cut and Generative Fill will replace it with content that makes sense for the image — from a longer bookshelf to a blue sky.
